📖 Bhagavad Gita · Chapter 2, Verse 47
"Karmaṇy evādhikāras te mā phaleṣu kadācana.
Mā karma-phala-hetur bhūr mā te saṅgo 'stv akarmaṇi."
"You have a right to perform your prescribed duties, but you are not entitled to the fruits of your actions. Never consider yourself the cause of the results of your activities, and never be attached to inaction."
This is one of the most profound verses in all of human philosophy. Krishna instructs Arjuna — and through him, all of humanity — that true liberation comes from performing one's duty without attachment to outcomes. This principle of Nishkama Karma forms the foundation of Karma Yoga. It does not mean indifference to results, but rather freedom from anxiety about them.
